\section{Conclusion}

We formulate attention-free language modeling as a separation between shared causal transport and narrow lexical specialization. The resulting candidate uses regular tensor operations and fixed-size incremental state. Final conclusions are deferred until all matched one-billion-token controls and held-out evaluations are complete. If the candidate does not outperform its controls, the negative result will still identify which integration choices fail under a matched budget and will narrow the claims accordingly.
